Former Penn State DB Adrian Amos to sign with divison rival

Adrian Amos started 38 straight games at Penn State.

Tyler DonohueMar 12th, 2019, 10:05 AM

Another Penn State football product will be earning his NFL paychecks in a new location next season. Defensive back Adrian Amos, who was a member of the Nittany Lions roster from 2011 through 2014, will join forces with a franchise that he'd previously competed against twice each year.

Amos is departing the Chicago Bears to sign with the Green Bay Packers, according to a Tuesday morning report from NFL.com's Ian Rapoport.

"An under-the-radar name... but a very, very good player," Rapoport observed while discussing the development on NFL Network. "It was really a question of whether or not the Bears were gonna franchise (tag) him. They decided not to. (Amos) was a free agent; expected to no longer be a free agent. From the Bears to rival Green Bay."

Amos was widely expected to be a coveted commodity on the open market. NFL free agency officially begins Wednesday at 3 p.m.

Financial terms were revealed by ESPN's Adam Schefter. It's a four-year, $37 million deal that is front-loaded with $21 million included in the first two years.

Amos, who turns 26 years old in April, has been a mainstay in the Bears defensive backfield since 2015, when Chicago selected him with a fifth-round draft pick.

He started all 16 games as a rookie, and duplicated that feat in 2018 while producing career-best totals for tackles (73), pass deflections (nine) and interceptions (three). Through four seasons with the Bears, Amos saw action in 60 games and started 56 of those contests.

Chicago boasted arguably the NFL's best defense last season, leaning on that unit en route to its first NFC North division championship since 2010. The Bears led the league in points allowed per game (17.7) and finished third in yards allowed per game (299.7).

The Packers were below average in both categories. Green Bay surrendered 25 points per game (ranked 22nd) and 354.4 yards per game (ranked 18th), finishing with the franchise's worst record (6-9-1) in a decade.

Amos will be counted on to help deliver a defensive turnaround.

"The Packers had a huge hole at safety," wrote 247Sports Packers reporter Bill Huber. "None of the safeties that ended last season on the roster had an interception. The starters to open last season were Ha Ha Clinton-Dix and Kentrell Brice. Clinton-Dix was traded to the Washington Redskins at midseason, despite a team-high three interceptions, and Brice suffered through a miserable season and was in and out of the lineup due to an injuries."

Amos compiled the third-best cumulative grade among NFL safeties when taking into account the last three seasons (2016-'18), according to Pro Football Focus. He trails only Earl Thomas of the Seattle Seahawks and Harrison Smith of the Minnesota Vikings, respectively.

In 49 career games with Penn State, Amos tallied 149 tackles, 19 pass breakups and seven interceptions. He finished his time in State College with 38 consecutive starts; the final 13 occurring as a member of coach James Franklin's first Nittany Lions squad.

Former Penn State teammate Jesse James, who was another fifth-round draft selection in 2015, is also undergoing professional relocation this week. It was reported Monday that the tight end is heading from the Pittsburgh Steelers to the Detroit Lions.

Amos arrived in Happy Valley as a three-star member of the 2011 Nittany Lions recruiting class out of Calvert Hall (Baltimore).
